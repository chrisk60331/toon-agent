## Summary of **data/laureate.json**

This file contains comprehensive data about Nobel Prize laureates from 1901 to 2025. Here's what the data includes:

### **Data Structure:**
- **Total Records**: 1,060+ laureate entries
- **Key Fields per Entry**:
  - Personal info: ID, firstname, surname, birth/death dates and locations
  - Biographical data: birthCountry, birthCountryCode, bornCity, diedCountry, gender
  - Prize information: year awarded, category (physics, chemistry, medicine, peace, literature, economics), share percentage, motivation/citation
  - Affiliations: institution name, city, country where work was performed

### **Prize Categories:**
1. **Physics** (annual, started 1901)
2. **Chemistry** (annual, started 1901)
3. **Medicine/Physiology** (annual, started 1901)
4. **Literature** (annual, started 1901)
5. **Peace** (annual, started 1901)
6. **Economics** (started 1969, added as official category)

### **Notable Features:**
- **Multiple Laureates**: Some individuals have won prizes in different years/categories (e.g., Marie Curie won Physics 1903 and Chemistry 1911)
- **Organizational Awards**: Organizations are included (Red Cross, UN agencies, campaigns)
- **Gender Distribution**: Includes female Nobel laureates (historically underrepresented before recent decades)
- **Geographic Coverage**: Laureates from over 100 countries worldwide
- **Time Range**: Early pioneers (1901) through very recent awards (2025)
- **Motivation Field**: Detailed citations explaining why each laureate received the prize

This is a rich dataset for analyzing Nobel Prize trends, gender representation, geographic distribution, and the evolution of scientific and peace accomplishments over 125+ years.

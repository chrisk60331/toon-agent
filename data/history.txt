## Summary of data/history.json

This file contains a comprehensive historical database with **795 recorded events** spanning from **1001 to 1100 CE** (early 11th century). Here are the key characteristics:

### **Structure:**
- Each event includes:
  - **Date** (ranging from 1001/02/06 to 1100/12/25)
  - **Description** of the historical event
  - **Language** (English)
  - **Category**: Events are organized by geography ("By place": Europe, Asia, Africa, Americas, etc.) and occasionally by topic (Religion, Arts, Technology, etc.)
  - **Granularity**: All set to "year" level

### **Geographic Coverage:**
- **Europe** - Dominant coverage including England, France, Germany, Poland, Scandinavia, Italy, and more
- **Asia** - China (Song Dynasty), Persia, Turkey (Seljuk), India, Korea, Vietnam, Japan
- **Africa** - North Africa, Egypt (Fatimids), Morocco (Almoravids), Ethiopia
- **Americas** - Limited entries about North American settlements and pre-Columbian cultures

### **Major Historical Themes Covered:**

1. **Political Changes**: Royal successions, coronations, battles, sieges, invasions
2. **Religious Events**: Papal elections, Church schisms (Great Schism of 1054), crusades (First Crusade 1095-1099), monastery foundations
3. **Military Conflicts**: Norman conquests (England 1066), Byzantine conflicts, crusades, Almoravid invasions
4. **Cultural & Scientific Achievements**: Construction of cathedrals, invention of movable type printing, astronomical observations
5. **Technology**: Paper money in China, printing technologies, mechanical innovations

### **Notable Events Highlighted:**
- **1066**: Norman Conquest of England
- **1054**: East-West Schism in Christianity
- **1095-1099**: First Crusade
- **1001-1087**: Various Viking, Byzantine, and Islamic dynastic conflicts

This historical database appears to be sourced from Wikipedia's "Years in history" pages and provides a rich chronological record of medieval world history.
